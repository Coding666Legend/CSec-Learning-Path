#### Introduction
**What is External Network PenTesting?**
> **External Network Penetration Testing** is a security assessment that simulates attacks from the public internet against an organization's internet-facing infrastructure to identify vulnerabilities that an external attacker could exploit.
> **Focused Heavily on :** OSINT and Information Gathering Techniques, attack surface enumeration

**What is Internal network PenTesting?**
> **Internal Network Penetration Testing** is a security assessment that simulates an attacker who has already gained access to an organization's internal network (e.g., through a compromised workstation, VPN account, or rogue device) to identify vulnerabilities, privilege escalation paths, lateral movement opportunities, and access to sensitive systems.
> **Focused Heavily on :** Active Directory Methodologies, privilege escalation, lateral movement, and post-exploitation.

**What is Web Application PenTesting?**
> **Web Application Penetration Testing** (Web App Pentesting) is the authorized process of simulating real-world attacks against a web application to identify, verify, and assess security vulnerabilities before attackers can exploit them.
> **Focused Heavily on :** web-based attacks and OWASP Top 10, business logic flaws

**What is Wireless PenTesting?**
>**Wireless Pentesting** is the security assessment of wireless networks and radio-based communication systems to identify vulnerabilities, validate security controls, and determine whether an attacker could gain unauthorized access or disrupt communications.
>**Focused Heavily on :** Wi-Fi security protocols, wireless reconnaissance, authentication attacks, rogue access points, client-side attacks, RF analysis, and wireless protocol security.

**What is Physical PenTest & Social Engineering?**
> **Physical Penetration Testing & Social Engineering** is a security assessment that evaluates an organization's physical security controls and human factors by simulating real-world attempts to gain unauthorized access to facilities, systems, or sensitive information through physical intrusion and psychological manipulation.
> **Focused Heavily on:** Physical access controls, badge and lock bypass techniques, tailgating, impersonation and pretexting, phishing, vishing, baiting, USB drops, human psychology, and security awareness validation.

**Report Writing Cheat Sheet**

>📅 Delivery
>Deliver the final report within ~1 week after the engagement.

>👔 Executive Summary
>Summarize the assessment in non-technical language.
>Highlight the overall risk, key findings, and business impact.

>💻 Technical Findings
>For each finding, include:
> - Description
> - Severity
> - Evidence / Proof of Concept (PoC)
> - Impact
> - Remediation

>🛠️ Recommendations
>Provide clear, practical, and prioritized remediation steps.
>Ensure recommendations are understandable by both executives and technical staff.

>Rule of Thumb: Finding → Evidence → Risk/Impact → Remediation

**Debrief Cheat Sheet**

>🤝 Purpose
>Walk the client through the report findings.
>Explain both technical and business impacts.

>👥 Audience
>Include technical and non-technical stakeholders.

>💬 Discussion
>Answer client questions.
>Clarify findings and remediation recommendations.
>Address concerns before the final report is issued.

>Goal: Ensure the client fully understands the findings and agrees on remediation before the final report is delivered.
